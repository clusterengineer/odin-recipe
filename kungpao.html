Kung Pao Chicken Recipe

Description

Kung Pao chicken is a beloved Chinese restaurant dish traditionally made with. While these can be found at Asian markets, there should be a version using staples available at most large supermarkets. This simplified recipe still captures the bold, signature flavors of the original and comes together quickly.

Ingredients

  • Soy Sauce
  • Dry Sherry
  • Cornstarch
  • Chicken
  • Balsamic Vinegar
  • Hoisin Sauce
  • Asian Toasted Sesame Oil
  • Crushed Red Pepper Flakes
  • Ground Ginger
  • Vegetable Oil
  • Red Bell Pepper & Celery
  • Garlic and Scallions
  • Peanuts or Cashews

Instructions

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together soy sauce, dry sherry, and cornstarch until the cornstarch is dissolved. Add hcicken and toss to coat
  2. Chop bell pepper, celery, scallions, and garlic.
  3. Prepare the sauce by whisking together balsamic vinegar, soy sauce, hoisin sauce, sesame oil, sugar, cornstarch, red pepper flakes, ground ginger, and water
  4. Heat a large skilled or wok over high heat. Add oil, bell pepper, celery, and salt. Cook for 5 minutes
  5. Add another half tablespoon of oil and crank up the heat. Toss in half the chicken and let it brown.
  6. Flip the chicken pieces then transfer them to the bowl with vegetables.
  7. Cook for 30 seconds, then add the reserved vegetables, chicken and sauce to the pan.
  8. Reduce the heat and cook for 30 seconds, until the chicken and vegetables are warmed through and sauce has thickened.
  9. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed, and serve with rice.
